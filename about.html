My name is Andrew Wigglesworth, a cybersecurity professional, community leader, and lifelong learner passionate about helping people understand technology and security. My journey into cybersecurity didn’t start in a classroom or at a help desk. It started with video games. Growing up, I was always intrigued about finding easter eggs, discovering glitches, making mods, and pushing the limits of getting the games to break. In addition to that I was always very technically savy so I enjoyed messing with different computers and devices. That curiosity eventually led me to pursue a Bachelor’s and Master’s degree in Cybersecurity and begin a career in information technology. Today, I work as a Systems Administrator while continuing to develop my skills in penetration testing, security operations, cloud technologies, and cybersecurity research. Outside of my professional work, I believe strongly in giving back to the cybersecurity community. I help support cybersecurity education, community outreach, mentoring, and professional development through organizations such as Blacks In Cybersecurity, where I hold the position of Global Ambassador Lead. I am constantly building new projects, expanding my homelab environment, participating in hands-on cybersecurity training, and documenting my learning journey through technical write-ups and portfolio projects. My long-term goal is to transition into a dedicated cybersecurity role where I can combine technical expertise, leadership, and community impact to help organizations defend against evolving threats while helping the next generation of cybersecurity professionals enter the field. When I’m not working on technology, you’ll usually find me playing basketball, traveling, attending cybersecurity conferences, volunteering in the community, or watching anime!
